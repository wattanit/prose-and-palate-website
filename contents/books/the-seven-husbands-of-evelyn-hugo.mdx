<p class="dropcap">The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo by Taylor Jenkins Reid is a masterfully crafted novel that pulls back the velvet curtain on Golden Age Hollywood to reveal a story of love, ambition, sacrifice, and the price of fame.</p>

## The Story

When reclusive Hollywood icon Evelyn Hugo finally decides to give her first interview in decades, she insists it be with Monique Grant, a struggling journalist at a failing magazine. Monique is baffled by this choice—she's never written about celebrities and has no connection to the legendary actress. But as Evelyn begins to tell her story, spanning seven decades and seven marriages, both women discover that their lives are more intertwined than either could have imagined.

## What Works Brilliantly

Reid's greatest achievement is Evelyn Hugo herself—a complex, flawed, and utterly compelling protagonist. Evelyn is neither purely sympathetic nor entirely unlikable; she's a woman who made difficult choices in impossible circumstances.

The novel's recreation of Hollywood's Golden Age feels authentic and immersive, weaving real historical events and figures into Evelyn's fictional narrative. And one of its greatest strengths is its portrayal of a same-sex relationship during an era when such love had to remain hidden: the relationship between Evelyn and Celia is tender, passionate, and heartbreaking.

  Never let anyone make you feel ordinary — least of all yourself.

</blockquote>

## Themes Explored

The novel turns over the cost of fame and success, love versus ambition, the masks we wear in public versus our private selves, and the power of storytelling and truth. It is character-driven historical fiction that earns every one of its 400 pages.

## Final Thoughts

*The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo* is a thoroughly engaging novel that succeeds on multiple levels. It's a page-turner with substance, a love story with depth, and a historical novel that feels relevant to contemporary issues. Perfect for book clubs, fans of historical fiction, and anyone who loves a good story about love and ambition.
