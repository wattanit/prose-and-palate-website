<p class="dropcap">We started this magazine because the internet had gotten very fast, and very loud, and we found ourselves wanting the opposite — something slow, considered, and made with the kind of care you can actually feel.</p>

There is a particular pleasure in a well-made thing, whether it's a cocktail measured to the millilitre, a spirit aged twelve quiet years, or a novel that refuses to hurry. Prose & Palate exists to pay attention to those things, and to the rooms and rituals around them.

## What We Review

We write about three things, and we try to do each of them properly:

- **Bars** — the rooms worth returning to, and the hospitality that makes you stay.
- **Drinks** — spirits, cocktails, and the quiet philosophy in a well-made pour.
- **Books** — fiction and essays that reward the reader who refuses to hurry.

<blockquote class="pullquote">
  The point was never to review everything. The point was to review the things worth slowing down for.
  <cite>— The Editors</cite>
</blockquote>

## How We Work

We pay for everything we review. We visit anonymously where we can. We do not accept payment for coverage, and we do not take ads from the places we write about. If we loved it, we'll tell you why. If we didn't, we'll tell you that too — but we'd rather spend our time on the things genuinely worth your attention.

### A Note on Scores

Our scores are on a ten-point scale, broken down by category. They're subjective, considered, and always accompanied by the reasoning behind them — never just a number in isolation.
