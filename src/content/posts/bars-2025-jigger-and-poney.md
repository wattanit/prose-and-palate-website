# Jigger & Pony - Singapore

*Asia's second-best bar looks exactly like every other bar. That is precisely the point.*

![](/images/posts/bars/2025/jigger-and-pony/2.jpg)

There is a particular kind of confidence that doesn't need to announce itself. Jigger & Pony, ranked No. 9 in the World's 50 Best Bars 2025 and second in Asia, sits inside the Amara Hotel in Singapore. From the outside, it is unassuming. Inside, you will find a straightforward bar, modest décor, and the kind of atmosphere you would recognise from any well-run cocktail bar in any city in the world. There are no theatrical flourishes, no statement installations, no mood lighting designed to tell you that this place is special. The bar does not need to tell you. It knows.

This restraint is not an accident. Jigger & Pony has built its reputation—and its place on the global stage—on a foundation of classical cocktails. Not "deconstructed" classics. Not avant-garde reinterpretations that bear only a philosophical resemblance to the originals. Just classics, done as well as they can be done. Sometimes with a single, precise twist. Nothing more.

![](/images/posts/bars/2025/jigger-and-pony/4.jpg)

## The Ordinary Bar That Is Anything But

Many bars earn their reputation through spectacle or novelty. Jigger & Pony earns its through pure, unadorned mastery. The bar seats roughly fifty people, which, given the volume of cocktail enthusiasts who make pilgrimages here, means the pacing feels deliberate but never hurried. Tables are close. Conversations carry. Yet somehow the energy stays relaxed.

The bartenders are the real differentiator in the atmosphere. They are friendly, talkative, and genuinely engaged—the kind who will ask what you usually drink and adjust accordingly. This is not a scripted "what are your flavor preferences?" checklist. It is an actual conversation. One bartender, on being told I preferred something less sweet and more aromatic in my Old Fashioned, simply tweaked the build without fanfare and delivered something closer to what I had in mind than I could have articulated myself. That level of attentiveness—responsive rather than performative—is rare at this volume of fame.

![](/images/posts/bars/2025/jigger-and-pony/3.jpg)

## The Courage of Classics

In the competitive world of high-profile cocktail bars, the easy path is the signature. A bar can mask technical inconsistency behind a novel ingredient, a theatrical presentation, or a concept that no one can fairly compare to anything else. A "signature cocktail" is a category of one. A classic, by contrast, is judged against every version you have ever tasted.

Jigger & Pony builds its menu almost entirely on that second standard. It takes real conviction to stake a world-class reputation on your ability to make a better Whiskey Sour, a better Old Fashioned, a better Manhattan than anyone else. And then to be right about it.

The **Yuzu Whiskey Sour** is the clearest illustration of their philosophy. It is faithful to the classic architecture—spirit, citrus, sweetener, egg white—with one addition: yuzu marmalade in place of the standard sweetener. The result does not feel like a reinvention. It feels like the drink found one more dimension it had always been missing. The yuzu introduces a clean, floral citrus brightness that cuts through the whisky's warmth without fighting it. But the defining quality is the texture. The egg white is shaken to a silky, cloud-like consistency, and there is not even a whisper of the eggy quality that lesser preparations leave behind. You find yourself wanting to keep drinking it simply because each sip is so perfectly assembled.

![](/images/posts/bars/2025/jigger-and-pony/10.jpg)

The **Old Fashioned** demonstrates what happens when a bar strips away every crutch. Three ingredients, a garnish, and nothing else. Adjusted to lean less sweet and more aromatic, it opens with a strong, assertive impact—the kind of first sip that reminds you why the drink has survived a century without modification. Then it mellows. By the mid-palate, a subtle sweetness emerges, and by the finish, the drink has resolved into something soft and lingering. It is one of the finest Old Fashioneds I have encountered.

![](/images/posts/bars/2025/jigger-and-pony/11.jpg)

The **Manhattan** receives a single, considered adjustment: cocoa and chocolate bitters alongside the standard aromatic bitters. Where a classic Manhattan can feel sharp and angular, this version carries more body. The chocolate rounds out the crisp vermouth character and gives the drink a smoothness that makes it deceptively easy to drink. The first sip still lands with intention, but the path through the glass is far more approachable.

![](/images/posts/bars/2025/jigger-and-pony/12.jpg)

## The Lighter Side

Not everything at Jigger & Pony needs to be spirit-forward. The **Pony Star Martini**—their play on the Porn Star Martini—is a case study in how to take an already popular drink and elevate its execution. The drink arrives refreshing and pleasingly tart, which is exactly what the original calls for. What makes this version memorable is the accompaniment: a passion fruit jelly, meant to be consumed whole. One bite, and a burst of concentrated sweet-sour flavour washes across the palate, providing the same counterpoint that a prosecco shot provides in the classic version, but with more impact and elegance. Once again, the egg white shaking is impeccable—a texture that feels like drinking a cloud.

![](/images/posts/bars/2025/jigger-and-pony/13.jpg)

## Final Thoughts

Jigger & Pony is proof that mastery, uncomplicated and undecorated, is its own kind of spectacle. To a stranger walking in off the street, it looks like every other bar. To anyone paying attention to what is in the glass, it becomes apparent almost immediately why it ranks where it does. The bar's willingness to build an international reputation on classics—on being right about the fundamentals rather than dazzling with the superficial—is an act of genuine confidence. And they are right to have it.
